Adjustment and Credit Tool

Project Overview

Support and Billing Rackers needed a better tool to give customers credits and adjustments. The previous tool was outdated and not user friendly. That much was clear. It’s design was beginning to show its age and wasn’t useful for cloud customer adjustments. For cloud customer adjustments and credits had to be done manually. This took a ton of time for Rackers to get these done. Business came to me with requirements to roll out a tool that will be featured on the company’s new internal framework called Encore.


Why am I building this?

To simply put it, I needed to help our customers faster with adjustments. Giving frontline Support Rackers a better tool to speed up the process will make the customers happy and free up Rackers time to do more run of the business tasks.

Once our BA gathered all of the requirements from a week long kaizen meeting, I was tasked to come up with workflows. Adjustment and credit tool (ACT) is a complex orchestration workflow engine in a way. At the surface you might picture or think that it is just a typical form that you see everyday. There are multiple backend systems and databases that it has to pull information. Also there is a Racker’s role to consider, threshold amounts and approval groups. To give some context I have attached an Invision link to show the workflows.


The Design

After I hammered out the workflows it was time to present some wireframes. I used a program called OmniGraffle to create low fidelity wireframes to show an overview of the ACT form to business. Once the business approves the wires or has feedback, I edit and meet again with business to get them to sign off on them. Within the form there is complex components associated with the creation of the request. For example depending on your role i.e a Support Racker or a Billing Racker you will see different form fields. I presented multiple use cases for each role. To begin with the Racker has to enter an account number. Once the account number is validated a dedicated or a cloud request is initiated. As the Racker fills out the form, one of the first inputs is the Reason Code field. This field drives the request and has a effect on what other fields are shown and populated. After the request form is filled out then there is a detail view showing what was in the form request. I then open up either Photoshop, Illustrator or Sketch to layout hi fidelity wireframes complete with Invision walkthroughs.
