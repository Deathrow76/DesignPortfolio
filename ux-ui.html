Rackspace Adjustment and Credit Tool

Project Overview

Support and Billing Rackers needed a better tool to give customers credits and adjustments. The previous tool was outdated and not user friendly. That much was clear. It’s design was beginning to show its age and wasn’t useful for cloud customer adjustments. For cloud customer adjustments and credits had to be done manually. This took a ton of time for Rackers to get these done. Business came to me with requirements to roll out a tool that will be featured on the company’s new internal framework called Encore.


Why am I building this?

To simply put it, I needed to help our customers faster with adjustments. Giving frontline Support Rackers a better tool to speed up the process will make the customers happy and free up Rackers time to do more run of the business tasks.


Workflows and Content

Workflow diagram showing different user flows.
Low fidelity wireframe of the ACT request form.
Low fidelity wireframe of the ACT detailed view.

Once our BA gathered all of the requirements from a week long kaizen meeting, I was tasked to come up with workflows. Adjustment and credit tool (ACT) is a complex orchestration workflow engine in a way. At the surface you might picture or think that it is just a typical form that you see everyday. There are multiple backend systems and databases that it has to pull information. Also there is a Racker’s role to consider, threshold amounts and approval groups. To give some context I have attached an Invision link to show the workflows.


The Design

After I hammered out the workflows it was time to present some wireframes. I used a program called OmniGraffle to create low fidelity wireframes to show an overview of the ACT form to business. Once the business approves the wires or has feedback, I edit and meet again with business to get them to sign off on them. Within the form there is complex components associated with the creation of the request. For example depending on your role i.e a Support Racker or a Billing Racker you will see different form fields. I presented multiple use cases for each role. To begin with the Racker has to enter an account number. Once the account number is validated a dedicated or a cloud request is initiated. As the Racker fills out the form, one of the first inputs is the Reason Code field. This field drives the request and has a effect on what other fields are shown and populated. After the request form is filled out then there is a detail view showing what was in the form request. I then open up either Photoshop, Illustrator or Sketch to layout hi fidelity wireframes complete with Invision walkthroughs.

Rackspace Customer Billing UI One Unified Billing Experience

Project Overview

Rackspace has great products and no one questions their Fanatical Support for them, but their customer portals and billing portals didn’t reflect that. These were designed over 5 years ago, back when Rackspace didn’t have people in UX positions. Sure there were web designers that worked on rackspace.com but not on development teams that built out these portals. Rackspace had a team that used Bootstrap 1.0 and styled it in Rackspace colors for dev teams to use to build with but to a customer with a dedicated account and a cloud account it looked like 2 different companies. There wasn’t a style guide with design patterns to follow when they built these. The customer didn’t have a seamless experience. Business finally listened to the customers and opened up a UX team and hired a well known person in the UX community to direct the department. To make a long story short my dev team received a illustrator file of the 1st draft of the new design system. I was put to task to create the new Customer Billing User Interface (CBUI). I was a pioneer in the company for this new look and customer experience.


Competitive Analysis

Competitive analysis of AWS & Google.

I took a deep dive into the way AWS and Google Cloud Platform handle their billing. I cherry picked what I thought was their best features and came up with a dashboard design that would fit our customers. The invoice details was what Rackspace was lacking and what I really focused on.


Customer Research & Information Gathering

Customer feedback captured with click analysis.
Mural card sorting exercise.

After doing competitive analysis on our competitors it was time to put lo-fiedilty wires together. I decided a dashboard for billing would be the best approach. Three different designs were presented to 5 customers with different personas: resellers, billing administrators and business owners.

Customer personas.

Site maps.

I captured all the feedback based upon what they would expect to see when looking at their billing. I used Mural for card sorting and designed our new wires against them. Our team then came up with hi-fi wires with clickable walkthroughs to present to dedicated and cloud customers again.


Customer Billing is Born

This is our final prototype that is currently in production. 10 months in the making. Research and information gathering, concept creation and iterative design, visual update and development. Very rewarding to see a concept that is brought to life and out in production providing great value and experience to our customers.

Rackspace Fill a Box Thanksgiving Food Drive

Project Overview

Rackspace needed a better way to let Rackers donate to the Thanksgiving food drive and there wasn’t an online portal to get the other Rackspace offices to donate.

I created a website that would let Rackers in offices country wide and the London office to donate and track the progress of the boxes donated.


Content

I reached out to the Rack Gives Back team to get content they had from previous food drives like pictures, the logo, and copy. I also wanted to understand what they wanted to get out of the site. After gathering all the content and proposed requirements I designed the site.

Total box count by location.

Fill a Box video with information about the program's initiative.

Design

I wanted the site to behave like an informational brochure that was dynamic in the way it tracked the boxes bought so as a company we could see how close we were to the goal. I leveraged Rackspace’s payment form web application that we used for customers to make secure donations on the site. This also was used to track the progress of the donations by location.
